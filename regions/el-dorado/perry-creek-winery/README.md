## Vineyards

At 2,400 feet elevation in the Fair Play AVA, Perry Creek farms 60 acres of vines in granitic soils. The estate uses cultural practices focused on ensuring consistently excellent quality fruit.

The combination of elevation, soil type, and microclimate creates ideal conditions for premium wine production.

## History

Perry Creek Winery was founded in 1991 by Michael Chazen, a garment industry executive who dreamed of making wine.

After building Perry Creek over 15 years, Chazen decided it was time to pursue other adventures. In September 2006, he sold the winery to Michele Wilms and Dieter Juergens, who continue the tradition of handcrafting fine wines at high elevation.

The winery has been "handcrafting fine wines at high elevation since 1995" (when commercial production began).

## Notes

Perry Creek is known as "Home of the elusive ZinMan" — their signature Zinfandel that has developed a devoted following.

The boutique winery is located just an hour from Lake Tahoe, making it a popular weekend destination for wine enthusiasts.
