## Vineyards

The estate vineyards are **organically farmed** at 2,500 feet elevation in the Fair Play AVA. The granitic soils and high altitude create intense, concentrated fruit.

The winery describes their approach: "Our wines are meticulously handcrafted using grapes nurtured in the granitic soils of our organically farmed estate."

## History

Jonathan Lachs and Susan Marks met while studying at UC Davis, where both were trained in viticulture and enology. After graduating in 1983, they worked at prestigious wineries in Napa Valley and the Santa Lucia Highlands before taking corporate jobs in Silicon Valley.

The pull of winemaking never left them. In 2000, they founded Cedarville Vineyard in Fair Play, returning to their passion.

Their debut wines made an immediate impact — Connoisseurs' Guide to California Wines named Cedarville their **"Newcomer of the Year" for 2001**, praising their "blockbuster, fruit-filled El Dorado County Syrahs and Zinfandels."

Susan Marie Marks has since passed, but Jonathan continues their shared vision, maintaining the commitment to quality and sustainability that defined their partnership.

## Notes

Cedarville represents the best of what happens when UC Davis-trained winemakers find perfect terroir. The combination of organic farming, high elevation, and meticulous craft produces some of Fair Play's most acclaimed wines.

Tastings are by appointment only, ensuring a personal experience with the winemaker.
