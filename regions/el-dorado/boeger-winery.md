**Estate Zinfandel** — Sourced from some of the county's oldest vines, showing classic Sierra Foothills character.

## Vineyards

The estate encompasses over 100 acres in the heart of the El Dorado AVA. The property includes historic buildings from the Gold Rush era, a redwood grove, stream, pond, park, and a notable historic wine cave built in 1872.

Boeger grows approximately 90% of the grapes used in their wines on the estate, ensuring complete quality control from vine to bottle.

## History

Greg and Sue Boeger established Boeger Winery in 1972, becoming **the first bonded winery in El Dorado County since Prohibition**. This pioneering move sparked the modern revival of winemaking in the Sierra Foothills.

The property itself dates to the Gold Rush era and includes an 1872 wine cellar that remains in use today. Some vines on the property trace their lineage back to the 1800s, making them among the oldest in the region.

Today, the winery is operated by Greg and his son Justin, who serves as winemaker. Boeger remains one of the largest and most respected producers in the Sacramento region.

## Notes

The grounds offer a magical atmosphere — visitors describe it as "stepping into another world." The combination of Gold Rush history, mature gardens, peaceful pond, and quality wines makes this essential visiting for anyone exploring El Dorado wine country.
