# Calaveras County

> Mark Twain's Gold Country — home of the Jumping Frog

## Overview

Calaveras County sits at the heart of the Sierra Foothills wine region, offering a perfect blend of Gold Rush history, small-town charm, and exceptional boutique wineries. The charming town of Murphys serves as the wine hub, with 20+ tasting rooms on Main Street alone.

## Climate

- **Days:** Warm, Mediterranean
- **Nights:** Cool due to elevation
- **Growing season:** Long, 260+ days
- **Rainfall:** Moderate

## Signature Varietals

- **Zinfandel** — Old-vine heritage
- **Barbera** — Italian tradition
- **Tempranillo** — Spanish varietals thriving
- **Albariño** — Spanish whites
- **Verdelho** — Portuguese heritage
- **Syrah** — Rhône style

## History

Wine has been made in Calaveras County since the Gold Rush era. In 1866, the Calaveras Chronicle noted the quantity of wine produced had doubled in each of the previous three seasons. Commercial viticulture flourished:

- **Red Mountain Vineyard** — Planted 1863, produced wines until the 1920s
- **Joseph Major's wine cellar** (Vallecito) — Later became Sciaccaluga and Fuzere wineries
- **John Heinsdorff** (Murphys) — Won First Award at 1869 California State Fair for best red wine
