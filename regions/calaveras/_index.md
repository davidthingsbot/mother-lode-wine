# Calaveras County

> Gold Rush heritage meets modern winemaking in the Sierra foothills

## Overview

Calaveras County — named for the Spanish word for "skulls" — sits in the heart of Gold Rush country. Best known for Mark Twain's "Celebrated Jumping Frog of Calaveras County," the region has a growing wine industry centered around the historic towns of Murphys and Angels Camp.

## Geography

Calaveras County does not have its own sub-AVA but falls within the larger Sierra Foothills AVA. Vineyards are concentrated around:

- **Murphys corridor** — Main tasting room destination
- **Angels Camp** — Historic mining town
- **Copperopolis** — Warmer, lower-elevation sites

## Murphys Wine Trail

The town of Murphys serves as the wine-tasting hub for Calaveras County, with numerous tasting rooms along Main Street. The walkable downtown makes it a popular destination for wine tourism.

## History

Winemaking in Calaveras County dates to the Gold Rush era, with vineyards planted throughout the Mother Lode. Like neighboring counties, the industry declined after phylloxera and Prohibition.

The modern revival began in the 1980s and 1990s. Ironstone Vineyards, established in 1989, became the region's anchor winery and a major tourism draw with its music amphitheater and Gold Rush museum.
